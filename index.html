Our story begins in the 1940s, a time when the world was rebuilding and reimagining the future after World War II.

In 1943, Warren McCulloch, a neuroscientist, and Walter Pitts, a logician, published a groundbreaking paper titled "A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity." They proposed a simplified model of how neurons in the brain might work, using electrical circuits to mimic neural behavior.

Continue

Their model represented neurons as simple on/off switches, laying the foundation for artificial neural networks by showing how complex functions could arise from interconnected simple units.

Artificial Neuron

An artificial neuron is a mathematical model inspired by the biological neuron. It processes inputs and produces an output based on a certain activation function.

Fast forward to 1958, Frank Rosenblatt, a psychologist, introduced the perceptron, the first artificial neural network capable of learning. The perceptron could recognize patterns and was initially used for image recognition tasks.

Perceptron

A perceptron is a type of artificial neuron that uses a weighted sum of its inputs and passes it through an activation function to produce an output.
